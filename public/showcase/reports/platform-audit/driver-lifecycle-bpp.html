Lifecycle

Driver Lifecycle: Registration → Settlement

Five stages from sign-up to payout, with document verification as the gate

1
Registration
Phone OTP via registration_token. 99.97% Android. 5.9% onboarded from dashboard. 4.4M person records, 745 fleet owners.
2
Document Onboarding
DL, Aadhaar, PAN, RC, Insurance, Fitness Cert. Two verification providers: IDFY (62% success) and HyperVerge (99.9% success). 14 document types across 7 vehicle categories.
3
Vehicle & Tier Assignment
Vehicle registered with RC. 21 service tiers from TAXI to AMBULANCE_VENTILATOR. Vehicles mapped to multiple eligible tiers. 78% cars, 16% autos, 6% bikes.
4
Active & Rides
18.8% of drivers active. Go online → receive search requests → quote → accept/reject. NY Club membership with 4-state lifecycle (ineligible → member → probation → revoked).
5
Settlement & Fees
YATRI_SUBSCRIPTION model. Avg platform fee ~11.47/ride, avg driver earnings ~466.75. 68.5% autopay, 29.6% manual. Coin rewards for ride milestones.

Findings

Critical Findings & Data Quality Gaps

Data Quality Issues

Vehicle Energy Type: 180+ Variants

Free-text entry causes "DIESEL", "Diesel", "DEISEL", "Disel", "Diseel", and even registration numbers entered accidentally. Needs enum enforcement.

Boolean Inconsistency Everywhere

onboarded_from_dashboard: True/TRUE/False/FALSE. is_new: True/TRUE/true/False/FALSE/false. Literal \N strings alongside NULLs in person table.

Vehicle Model: 24% Unknown/Empty

378K empty + 111K "Unkown" (misspelled). Vehicle color has dozens of white variants (PEARL ARCTIC WHITE, SUPERIOR WHITE, ARCTIC WHITE, WHITE, White...).

trip_category Format Migration

Mixed JSON objects ({"tag":"OneWay"}) and underscore-delimited strings (OneWay_OneWayRideOtp) in the same column. Schema migration in progress.

Operational Gaps

Go-Home: 85% Failure Rate

54,149 FAILED vs 4,201 SUCCESS. Only 4,384 drivers have home locations set. Feature needs significant matching improvement or expectation management.

Driver Accept Rate: Only 2.6%

Of 33.6M driver requests (3d): 49% rejected, 28% pulled by system, 20% no response. Funnel drops from 3.2M searches to 991K completed rides.

IDFY Verification: 29% Failure

IDFY: 5,839 completed, 2,754 failed, 383 pending. HyperVerge: 2,287 success, 2 pending (99.9%). IDFY handles DL + RC; HyperVerge only RC.

Unpopulated Columns Across Tables

booking.payment_mode: 100% NULL. estimate.fare_product_type: 100% NULL. ride.tip_amount: 100% NULL. estimate.date index corrupted.

Priority Action Items

1
Data normalization: Enforce enums for energy_type, color, vehicle_class. Fix boolean casing. Clean \N strings.
2
Accept rate optimization: 2.6% accept rate means massive compute waste on 97.4% failed dispatches
3
Go-Home feature: 85% failure rate needs algorithmic rework or feature repositioning
4
Column cleanup: Remove or populate 100%-NULL columns; fix estimate table index corruption
The platform serves enormous scale with rich driver lifecycle management. The primary risks are data quality debt (180+ energy type variants, boolean inconsistencies) and the 2.6% driver accept rate driving a 10:1 compute-to-conversion ratio in the matching engine.
