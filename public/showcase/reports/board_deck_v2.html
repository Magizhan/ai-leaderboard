Execution Methodology: How We Get to 993K/day

Mode 1: Defend & Compound

KA 154K→195K (+27%) • WB 29K→40K (+38%)
  • Driver retention: Fix 48% fee collection (₹2.7Cr leakage). Coin redemption at 26% — increase to 60%
  • Conversion: 2.6% accept rate → 4% via ML allocation (H3 hex data ready, 170M rows)
  • Expansion within KA: Mangalore, Hubli, Mysore growth — already at 12 cities
  • WB deepening: Siliguri, Asansol, Durgapur active. Add tier-2 coverage
Risk: Low. Organic growth in proven markets. Revenue: ₹33 Cr (60% of total)

Mode 2: Land & Expand

BT 13.5K→67K (+5x) • ROI 6K→26K (+4x)
  • BT NCR playbook: 854→13,555 in 12 wks validated. Driver self-onboard via zero-commission. Sustain 15% MoM
  • BT Gujarat: Ahmedabad 4.7K, expanding Surat/Rajkot. Same playbook as NCR
  • ROI (TN/KL/OD/TG): Start with enabler partnerships. Govt transport dept tie-ups before ops
  • No city ops overhead: Subscription model = drivers pay ₹25/day. No per-ride margin pressure
Risk: Medium. NCR proven, GJ ramping. ROI unproven. Revenue: ₹8 Cr (14%)

Mode 3: Govt-Led Scale

Chennai 85K→400K • Others 3K→240K
  • Chennai One: MTC full route digitization + CMRL + Suburban rail. Depot push already shows 23K passes/day when activated
  • Replication model: One state transport contract = entire city. Software-only (no Chalo-style hardware)
  • Pipeline: Odisha (OY), Bharat Taxi (BT PT), Kerala (KS), Bangalore (NY). Each contract ~3-6 month ramp
  • NCMC alignment: Delhi Saheli card, MSRTC mandate — national tailwind for digital PT
Risk: Medium-High. Chennai proven, others depend on govt timelines. Revenue: ₹3.5 Cr (6%) but highest growth
Revenue split by mode: Mode 1 (60%) funds operations • Mode 2 (14%) proves national scale • Mode 3 (6% now, but largest TAM) proves infra narrative
Global R&D (<2% cost) kept as BECKN protocol experiment — not in critical path
