React Migration: What We Lost

The PureScript app had mature Purple Ride features (~180 files). The React migration (Jun 2024) broke or dropped several.

PureScript Had (Working)

  • "Purple Ride Champion" confetti badge for drivers
  • Disability onboarding banner on customer home
  • Driver education banner with video carousel
  • Call button disabled for hearing-impaired riders
  • Chat auto-skip for blind riders
  • Auto-scroll disabled for low-vision users
  • Repeat-ride timer skipped for blind users
  • Per-vehicle, per-city disability tutorial videos
  • Post-ride assistance survey

Lost or Broken in React

  • Both banners commented out — onboarding + driver education removed Sep 2024
  • setFocus() commented out — screen readers can't focus modals
  • 15+ accessibilityLabel={undefined} — screen readers silenced
  • Hearing/blind guards may be missing — call-disable & chat-skip were PureScript-only
  • DISABILITY_NAME lost on logout — React login may not re-hydrate
  • 3 urgent video fix commits needed within weeks of migration

Jun 2024 migration: 4,152 lines changed in Flow.purs. No React equivalent created for the PureScript disability popup flow. Purple features were deprioritized during the transition.

Root Cause: Disability Tag Fragility

The disability tag passes through 6+ layers — and keeps breaking

The Pipeline

Rider Profile → SearchRequest
SearchRequest → Beckn Protocol
Beckn → BPP SearchRequest
BPP Search → Select
Select → Booking
Booking → Ride

Dec 2024: 12 commits in 7 days just to fix disability tag propagation. One change broke it, fix broke something else, then had to add it back.

No Integration Tests

No Accessibility Testing

Real Experience: What Can Be Better

A developer with disability shared their ride experience (video) — and it shows the gaps

The Experience Today

  • Driver doesn't know what to do when they arrive — no training video played before accepting
  • No contextual guidance in local language at pickup
  • Communication barriers persist — drivers still try to call deaf riders
  • No proactive assistance — rider has to explain their needs every time
  • Nano training videos exist but are not shown at the right moment

What's Missing for Riders

  • No real-time status in accessible format (vibration patterns for blind users)
  • No emergency SOS designed for PwD (deaf user can't call 112)
  • No preferred driver option to rebook a good driver
  • No scheduled/recurring rides for regular commutes (hospital, work)
  • No companion ride mode for caregivers
  • No wheelchair-accessible vehicle filter
  • App Store doesn't declare accessibility features

Suggested Improvements — Driver Side

  • Nano video before acceptance: Show 30s disability-specific video in local language before driver accepts, not after
  • Pickup guidance card: Visual checklist at pickup (horn once for blind, text for deaf, assist wheelchair)
  • Local language training: All 14 supported languages, not just English/Kannada
  • Driver opt-in + certification: Trained drivers get priority for purple rides + monetary bonus
  • Post-ride feedback loop: "Did you offer assistance?" with real consequences for scoring
  • Purple Ride Champion badge: Bring back the confetti badge from PureScript!

Suggested Improvements — Rider Side

  • Haptic navigation: Vibration patterns for ride status (assigned, arriving, arrived)
  • Voice-first booking: "Book my usual ride" via voice command
  • Accessible SOS: One-tap emergency with pre-filled disability info sent to responders
  • Favourite drivers: Rebook drivers who provided great assistance
  • Scheduled rides: Recurring bookings for hospital/therapy/work commutes
  • Ride rating for accessibility: Separate accessibility rating from general ride rating
  • Onboarding video for riders: "How Purple Rides works" in accessible format

Chennai One: The Multimodal Accessibility Opportunity

India's first unified ticketing app — but disability advocates say it's not accessible enough

What Advocates Are Saying

  • Screen reader incompatible — Freedom of Movement Coalition and Prof. Raghuram (Disability Rights Alliance) flagged issues before launch
  • Sathish Kumar (disability activist): "The app only asks whether the user is disabled but does nothing meaningful with that information"
  • No low-floor bus marking — wheelchair users can't identify accessible buses
  • No station accessibility data — no lift/escalator info, no accessible entrance mapping
  • Apple App Store: "developer has not indicated accessibility features"

The Physical Reality

10 of 3,740 MTC buses are disabled-friendly (0.3%). Only 9 of 158 suburban railway stations have wheelchair facilities. The app can't fix infrastructure, but it can help navigate it.

What We Can Do — Purple + Chennai One

  • Accessible ticketing UI: Apply Purple Rides accessibility fixes to the ticketing flow
  • Low-floor bus tags: Mark accessible buses in the route planner
  • Station accessibility map: Crowdsource lift/ramp/entrance data per station
  • Multimodal Purple journey: Auto → metro → bus with accessibility support at each leg
  • Real-time alerts: "Lift at X station is out of service" for wheelchair users
  • First/last mile: Purple Ride auto to/from metro station

Why This Matters

Chennai One is high-visibility. Media has already flagged accessibility gaps. If we make it the most accessible public transport app in India, it becomes the template for every city. Enable India can help us get it right.
